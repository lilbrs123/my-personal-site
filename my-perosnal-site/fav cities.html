Favourite Cities

Since I was little these were my Favourite cities to see.

Mexico City

A marriage of neo-Gothic and Spanish-colonial architecture, the city — located 170 miles northwest of Mexico City — is a design lover's dream. "The mystery of what the houses look like behind the doors is intriguing," said one reader. Described as simply "magical" by more than one respondent, San Miguel de Allende also captivated readers with its history and growing culinary clout. Our respondents also gushed over the plentiful sights — "everywhere you look there are amazing sights and beauty and history" — and remarked on how walkable and easy-to-navigate the city is. From the rooftop restaurants to the central El Jardín and the Museo Histórico, San Miguel de Allende has mesmerized our readers, one of whom summarized: "San Miguel is an incredible city, very beautiful, historic, with friendly people, fantastic food and restaurants."

Istanbul

Istanbul, the largest city in Turkey, straddles Europe and Asia, making it a unique and vibrant metropolis. With a rich history that dates back over 2,500 years, Istanbul has served as the capital of three empires – Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman – and is filled with architectural and cultural treasures that reflect its storied past. Visitors can explore the magnificent Hagia Sophia, the stunning Blue Mosque, or take a stroll through the bustling Grand Bazaar, one of the oldest and largest covered markets in the world. The Bosphorus Strait, which runs through the city, offers stunning views of the city's skyline and connects the Sea of Marmara to the Black Sea. Istanbul is a city that seamlessly blends its ancient past with its modern present, making it a fascinating destination for travelers from all over the world.In addition to its historical and cultural landmarks, Istanbul is also known for its vibrant and diverse culinary scene. Visitors can sample traditional Turkish dishes such as kebabs, meze, and baklava, as well as enjoy international cuisine at trendy cafes and restaurants.

Tokyo

Tokyo, the bustling capital city of Japan, is a vibrant and modern metropolis that seamlessly blends traditional culture with cutting-edge technology. As one of the world's most populous cities, Tokyo offers endless opportunities for visitors to explore its diverse neighborhoods, from the neon-lit streets of Shinjuku and the trendy shops of Harajuku to the serene gardens of the Imperial Palace. Visitors can sample delicious Japanese cuisine at local restaurants and markets, and explore the city's many museums and galleries to learn about Japan's rich history and contemporary art scene. With its efficient public transportation system and friendly locals, Tokyo is a city that is easy to navigate and welcoming to visitors from all over the world.Despite its fast-paced and high-tech environment, Tokyo also offers plenty of opportunities for relaxation and contemplation. The city is home to many beautiful parks and gardens, including Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden and Yoyogi Park, where visitors can enjoy a peaceful respite from the urban hustle and bustle. With its blend of modernity and tradition, Tokyo is a city that has something for everyone. Its efficient transportation, friendly locals, and endless opportunities for exploration make it a must-visit destination for travelers from around the world.

Trieste

Trieste is a charming city located in the northeastern region of Italy, bordering Slovenia. This port city has a unique blend of Italian, Austrian, and Slavic culture and history, making it a fascinating destination for visitors. Trieste is home to beautiful architecture, including the grand Piazza Unità d'Italia, which faces the sea and is one of the largest public squares in Europe. The city is also known for its coffee culture, with numerous historic cafes serving delicious espresso and pastries. Visitors can explore the winding streets of the old town and visit landmarks such as the castle of Miramare, which overlooks the sea and was once the residence of the Habsburg family. Trieste is also a gateway to the region's stunning natural beauty, including the nearby Carso plateau and the Gulf of Trieste, with its crystal-clear waters and rocky beaches. With its rich history, culture, and natural beauty, Trieste is a hidden gem that is well worth a visit.
