Favourite Spots

“It is not down in any map; true places never are.” ~ Herman Melville.

Badshahi Masjid

Badshahi Mosque is a magnificent landmark located in Lahore, Pakistan. Built in 1673 during the Mughal era, it is considered one of the largest and most iconic mosques in the world. The mosque features stunning architectural details, including marble domes, minarets, and intricate tile work. It has the capacity to accommodate over 100,000 worshipers, making it one of the most significant places of worship in Pakistan. The Badshahi Mosque is not only a place of religious significance but also an important tourist attraction that draws visitors from all over the world.Today, the Badshahi Mosque remains an important cultural and religious landmark in Pakistan, and it attracts thousands of visitors every year. It has also been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which has helped to preserve its historic and cultural significance for future generations.

Blue Mosque

The Blue Mosque, also known as Sultan Ahmed Mosque, is an iconic landmark located in Istanbul, Turkey. Built in the early 17th century, the mosque is famous for its striking blue tiles that adorn the interior walls, which give it its nickname. The mosque was commissioned by Sultan Ahmed I, who wanted to build a place of worship that would rival the grandeur of the nearby Hagia Sophia. The mosque's architecture is a beautiful blend of Islamic and Byzantine styles, with a large central dome, six towering minarets, and intricate tile work. Today, the Blue Mosque is still an active place of worship and a popular tourist attraction, drawing visitors from all over the world who come to admire its beauty and learn about its rich history.

Tokyo Tower

Tokyo Tower is a popular landmark located in the heart of Tokyo, Japan. Standing at 332.9 meters tall, it is one of the tallest towers in the world and is a prominent feature of the Tokyo skyline. The tower was built in 1958 as a symbol of Japan's post-war resurgence and was modeled after the Eiffel Tower in Paris, France. Visitors can take an elevator to the observation deck, which offers breathtaking views of Tokyo's bustling streets and skyline. The tower also has several restaurants, souvenir shops, and even a wax museum, making it a popular tourist destination. Today, the Tokyo Tower remains an important cultural icon in Japan and is a must-visit attraction for anyone traveling to Tokyo.

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is an iconic mountain located in Honshu, Japan. It is the highest peak in Japan, standing at 3,776 meters tall, and is one of the country's most recognizable symbols. Mount Fuji is a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts, who come to climb its slopes and take in its stunning views. The mountain is also considered a sacred site in Japanese culture, and it has been the subject of many works of art and literature throughout history. In 2013, Mount Fuji was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural significance and natural beauty. Whether viewed from afar or climbed to its summit, Mount Fuji is a must-see destination for anyone visiting Japan.
